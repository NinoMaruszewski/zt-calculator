    Procedure to Calculate Device ZT from materials properties

    The efficiency of a Thermoelectrc Device is typically reported as a function of ZT (Device Figure of Merit)

     

     

    Here we show you how to calculate ZT from thermoelectric materials properties that are temperature dependent as described in [1]. You will need the thermoelectric properties of your material as a function of temperature from Tc (cold side temperature) to Th (hot side temperature).

     

    1. Place your data into a spreadsheet.

    Create a spreadsheet with the following header:
    Tempparture (K),r (mOhm cm),Seebeck (uV/K),Thermal Conductivity (milliwatts/cm*K)).
    After that, place your data into the spreadsheet in the appropriate columns, and save it as a CSV file.


    2. Now, upload your data.

    Click on the Choose File button below and upload your file. If that fails, try using the pastebox.

    CSV Preview

    3. Calculate!

    Now click the button below to calculate ZT!


    References

    The following journal article gives a complete description of the calculation for reference

    [1] G. J. Snyder, A. H. Snyder "Figure of Merit ZT of a Thermoelectric Device Defined from Materials Properties" Energy and Environmental Science 10, 2280 (2017)

     

    Further details and derivation of the calculations can be found in

    [2] G. J. Snyder "Thermoelectric Power Generation: Efficiency and Compatibility" Chapter 9, in Thermoelectrics Handbook: Macro to Nano edited by D. M. Rowe, CRC Press (2006).

     

    [3] A python and Java Script version of the calculator written by A. H. Snyder can be downloaded from GitHub. Link to the JavaScript version of the calculator.
